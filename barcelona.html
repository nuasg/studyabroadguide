Barcelona

Spanish Language and Summer Culture Program
Receiving NU Credit
  • You will receive 3 Spanish credits
  • Will fulfill Weinberg language requirement

    • Requirements
  • Classes will be Grammar, Culture, and Conversation
  • Culture and Conversation classes are all NU Students
  • You will place into classes based on level
    • Classes
  • All classes are taught in Spanish
  • Classes go from 9:30-4 everyday with a 2 hour break for lunch
  • Classes are easy, similar to high school Spanish classes

    • Homework
  • Homework is easy and quick, normally not more than 30 minutes and can be completed during breaks
  • You live in dorms, the whole program lived in the same building
  • Each room has a kitchenette, a desk, and a bathroom
  • Most people stay with a roommate; you can get a single but they cost more
  • You room with someone from the same program
  • There is a roommate survey before you leave
  • Dorm is close to beach, close to school, close to clubs
  • You pay for laundry
  • Other American programs as well as native Spanish speakers living in the dorm

    • Eating

    Eating out is easy, there are lots of options

    • Eating out is easy, there are lots of options
    • For lunch, students either buy from the school or bring their own
    • Groceries are not very expensive, the first trip will be the most expensive because you have to stock up
    • Eating out usually ranged from 10-15 euros
    • There is not a lot of places for late night food, most places close around 1 am
    • Alisa the program director is also great with suggestions, so when in doubt ask her
    • From Students who lived in dorms:
      • We usually ate out 3-4 times a week
      • Spend some time walking around, you can find some great places, most tapas places are really good
      • Don't tip, leave small change like pennies
      • I wish we had eaten out more

    Night Life

    Clubs

  • Wear nicer clothes, not super dressed up but kind of preppy
    • Boys: Button down and jeans
    • Girls: Nicer sundress, skirt and top
  • Most of the clubs were filled with college aged kids
  • Shaz's list is great; sign up for clubs beforehand, get to the place by a certain time and you won't get turned away
  • Guys are going to pay a cover charge, about 10 euros depending on the bouncer
  • Don't walk home alone. Being in Barcelona is like being anywhere else; you'll be fine, just don't take unnecessary risks.

    • Specific clubs
  • Razzmatazz is college club, younger crowd
  • Opium is a common favorite, crowd tends to be more mid twenties
  • Chapitos is a great shots bar, it is very crowded and most people pregame (it is 2 euros per shot)

    • Timing

    Clubs don't start getting crowded until 1am

    Travelling

    Travelling within Barcelona
  • Chapitos Bar
  • Sagrada Família
  • Park Guell has great views of the city
  • Casa Batllo (you will visit during the trip)
  • Bocarilla is a huge market, has great fresh food and candies
  • Camp Nou, FC Barcelona's stadium
  • Fountains at Montjuic has a light show at night
  • Sitges is a little beach town; you can get there by a 45 minute train ride. Perfect day trip location

    • Weekend Trips
  • You will have lots of time to travel so take advantage of it
  • The program will take you to Madrid
  • The program also sponsors a day trip to Montserrat
  • Italy is a great weekend trip to take, visit Milan and Florence
  • Budapest is another place to visit

    • Travel Tips

    Cab Ride:
    60 euros to the airport
    Plaza Catalona Bus:
    5 euros to the airport
    Ryanair:
    Cheap flights
    Subways:
    Easy to use; use for farther attractions
    In General:
    People are very laid back and don't travel with urgency so people tend to move slowly
