Paris

"Living in and getting to know Paris - it was my first time in Paris. I now want to live in Paris for a few years at some point in my life. I love fashion and food and Paris is a perfect place to be for that. The culture is great to discover. There are enough things to do that you can do something new each day."

IFSA Butler

Receiving NU Credit
  • You can receive up to 5 credits
  • Credit earned by past students: credits for a global health minor, history distro, language elective, BIP credits, Legal studies credits

    • Other Requirements
  • On top of that, you take a French class. There are many levels of French you can take, which you are placed into in the beginning. This class completely depends on what level you are in and who your lecturer is. There are worksheets for this class.

    • Classes Taken by Previous Students
  • Classes about Public Health Systems are interesting
  • Economics of Health was a lot different than classes in the USA
    • Lectures
  • Classes are taught in English, except for French.
  • The lecturers were all prominent people in their fields and very interesting to hear from.
  • Many guest speakers come in from around the country and because they come from far distances, they only have one day to teach all of their information. This results in long, 6-7 hour, lectures. No discussions accompany these lectures and there are periodic breaks. There are about four of these long lectures a week.

    • Homework
  • There are many assigned readings throughout the quarter but most students chose to do them at the very end of the semester. Overall, there is only a lot of work during midterms, finals, and for group projects
    • For the ISFA Butler program, you can choose to live with a host family or in dorms

    Home Stay
  • This experience depends on what kind of person you are and what family you are placed with. Some people love it and others hate it.
    • Dorm
  • Pros: everyone is near each other, near the school
  • Cons: small rooms, you have a roommate, bunk beds, strict rules for having guests over, have to check in with a guard, no kitchen, mice in the kitchen, spotty wifi
  • Northwestern students are of all different backgrounds and majors. Most are global health minors.
  • In the French class, you are with European students learning French.
  • You mainly hang out with people who are in the program which are mostly Northwestern students and a few French kids. The French kids partake in the program to enhance their English.

    • Eating

    Easy to eat unhealthy because there are so many yummy pastry shops

    • Eating out is somewhat difficult because it is hard to know what you are ordering from a French menu. You order in French. It is a big production to eat out and takes a lot time. It is very different from the hurried pace of American restaurants.
    • Spending per week varies greatly depending on how you chose to eat. Can be anywhere from $70 to $250.
    • Breakfast is usually espresso and a pastry (for French people).
    • Lunch is usually a baguette sandwich
    • Drinking water is free but comes in tiny "shot-glass sized" cups. Bring your own water bottle or you will be perpetually thirsty. There is no ice.
    • Try macaroons from La Duree or Pierre Hemme, go into other districts where the food is less touristy, more affordable and delicious
    • Tipping: you do not have to tip.
    • From people who lived in the dorms:
      • You eat out a lot
      • Buy frozen food that you can microwave
      • Buy pre-made food to go

    Time of meal

    Dinner is usually after 8pm with most restaurants opening up after 7pm

    Night Life

    Pregame

  • Pregame by the Seine River when it was warm out
  • Typical Night Out: pregame by the Seine, go to the bars at 10pm, go to the clubs are 11:30 (when it is cheaper to get in)

  • Bars and clubs are very expensive
  • Some clubs are very high end and hard to get in to, others are chill and you can dress casually.
  • Bring cab fare! The Metro stops run till 1am, use trial and error to find good places.
