Copenhagen

Danish Institute for Study Abroad (DIS)
  • Classes are easier than Northwestern classes
  • You spend about 15 hours a week in class and 5 to 10 hours a week on homework
    • You have a few options when comes to living:

    Home Stay
  • Host parents and brother
  • Involves commuting to school, but Denmark has a good train system

    • Dorms
  • Called DRS and available for all DIS students
  • Closest option to school

    • Living & Learning Communities
  • These are like residential colleges and are centered around themes (culinary, fine arts, outdoorsy)
  • Only for American students
    • Kollegiums
  • Students from multiple schools live in the same dorm

    • Folkehojskole
  • These are like boarding schools, and are the best option to intermix with Danish students

    • Danish Roommate
  • Apartment style living, Danish students are older (23/24 years old at least)
    • All students in this program and at this institute are American students studying abroad

    Pros
  • Easy credit transfer
  • Accommodates travel (there are weeks with no school)
  • Classes go on field trips
  • Classes are taught in English

    • Cons
  • It is can be hard to meet Danish students (there are some opportunities but they are not heavily advertised)

    • Eating

    Check out the pizza place near the school and 7/11 for some cheap eats!

    Day-to-Day

  • Expect to spend about $15 when going out to eat
  • Students get a food stipend
  • When in a home stay, it is normal not to spend anything during a week

    • Places to eat

    Restaurants
    Dalle Valle: Decent cheap eats
    Sankt Peder's Bageri: Copenhagen's oldest bakey. Cinnamon Roll Wednesdays

    Grocery Stores
    Netto: cheapest
    Fo-Tex: a bit nicer
    Aldi
    Superbest: sort of like Whole Foods

    Night Life

    Club

  • Kulorbar: Right near one of the dorms and many students go here
  • Check out the meat packing district for a crazy night. It is and up and coming area for nightlife

    • Timing

    Go out super late
    People start pregaming at 11 pm and stay out until 5 - 6 am
