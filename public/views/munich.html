Munich

NU-SAO: Finding Nano

Level of Difficulty - Difficult
  • The work content is challenging, but grading is not too harsh if you put in a decent amount of effort.
  • Students got to visit several companies and research centers for the Nanotech class.
  • German "tutors" were available to befriend, as well as to ask for help and advice.

    • Receiving NU Credit
  • You can receive up to 3 credits
  • All of the program participants are put into Apartment-style hotels; this encourages students to get to know each other.
  • It is possible to choose your own roommate, but otherwise you will be randomly paired.
  • Breakfast is provided.

    • Eating

    Germany is not a good place to be a vegetarian

    • Weekly spending: 40-50 euros
    • Groceries are cheap, providing for a fairly low-cost living. Eating out will not be too big of a problem as well because it's very affordable.
    • Cake and coffee are customary on Sundays
    • Most places are closed on Sundays
    • Fresh produce comes in on Mondays and Thursdays
    • Breakfast food is usually cold cut meat
    • If you go to Starbucks, make sure you specify to put more espresso shots in, otherwise they'll only put in one or two
    • Recommendations to try:
      • Bratwurst
      • Currywurst
      • Pork knuckle
      • Ice Kaffe (coffee with ice cream)
      • Radler (half beer half lemonade)
      • Apfelschorle (apple juice and sparkling water)
      • Spezi (nonalcoholic beverage)

    Night Life

    What to wear

  • When you go out, try to look more hip and casual
  • Do not wear dress shirts; it'll come across as though you're too try-hard
  • Avoid dressing like a tourist

    • Legal Age
  • 21+ for clubs, but they don't card strictly
  • 18+ for hard alcohol
  • 16+ for beer and wine
  • There are no open container laws - it's legal to drink in public! Just make sure to be smart about it.

    • Bars and Clubs
  • Bar scene is completely different from the club scene (clubs are very techno, no pop music)
  • A lot of people are under the influence of drugs, so be aware
  • Women - Be very wary of men who thigh-slap you - as this is a sexual advance
  • As Germans tend to be xenophobic, speak as much German as you can at clubs, not English

    • Fun fact
  • Beer is cheaper than bottled water

    • Typical night

    11pm/Midnight: Go to bar
    2/3am: Head to a nightclub
    4/5am: Get home

    • Transportation

    Inner Loop
    The university gives you tickets that gets you around the inner loop.

    Trains
    Trains are closed from 1 to 4am - plan accordingly. Also, don't talk too loudly on the trains, as they will glare at you

    Last Minute Tips

    Hair-care
    Germany has hard running water, and makes your hair really rough, so bring hair-care products.
    Bottled Water
    Bottled water is very expensive, and you typically cannot get tap water for free at restaurants, so make sure you bring a water bottle (it is safe to drink from taps).
    Safety
    There are a lot of scammers - avoid women and little children asking you to do something. They'll try to take things from you. Don't feel bad about not helping them.
    Communication
    Consider getting an international data roaming plan for phones. Also, try using Viber, WhatsApp, or other messaging apps while you have wifi.
