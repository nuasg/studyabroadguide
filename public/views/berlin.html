Berlin

NU-IPD - Global City in the Center of Europe

Level of Difficulty - Difficult
  • The program is comprised of 50% Northwestern and 50% the German university
  • The Northwestern component is fairly time intensive, but not at all impossible
  • Students have about 3 hours of class a day, and approximately 8 hours of work to do outside of class

    • Receiving NU Credit
  • You will receive 2 credits
  • You will be living in a youth hostel, living with about 300 people
  • A slight downside to this is that there is only 1 kitchen, 1 laundry machine, and 1 dryer unit for the whole building

    • Eating

    Germany is not a good place to be a vegetarian

    • Students received stipends for breakfast
    • Weekly spending: 30 euros if you do not eat out, 50 euros if you do
    • You won't have to spend too much if you can cook for yourself in the kitchens provided, as ingredients are cheap
    • Cake and coffee are customary on Sundays
    • Most places are closed on Sundays
    • Fresh produce comes in on Mondays and Thursdays
    • Breakfast food is usually cold cut meat
    • If you go to Starbucks, make sure you specify to put more espresso shots in, otherwise they'll only put in one or two
    • Recommendations to try:
      • Bratwurst
      • Currywurst
      • Pork knuckle
      • Ice Kaffe (coffee with ice cream)
      • Radler (half beer half lemonade)
      • Apfelschorle (apple juice and sparkling water)
      • Spezi (nonalcoholic beverage)

    Night Life

    What to wear

  • When you go out, try to look more hip and casual
  • Do not wear dress shirts; it'll come across as though you're too try-hard
  • Avoid dressing like a tourist

    • Legal Age
  • 21+ for clubs, but they don't card strictly
  • 18+ for hard alcohol
  • 16+ for beer and wine
  • There are no open container laws - it's legal to drink in public! Just make sure to be smart about it.

    • Bars and Clubs
  • Bars: Dr. Pong (good for weekdays), Bar 55 (liter cocktails), Winrei (all you can drink wine bar for 2 euros, but the catch is that they ask you to pay the amount you think you consumed at the end)
  • Clubs: Berghain (most famous club and is very exclusive, not surprising to find a 3 hour line where you may even get turned away at the door
  • Bar scene is completely different from the club scene (clubs are very techno, no pop music)
  • A lot of people are under the influence of drugs, so be aware
  • Women - Be very wary of men who thigh-slap you - as this is a sexual advance
  • As Germans tend to be xenophobic, speak as much German as you can at clubs, not English

    • Fun fact
  • Beer is cheaper than bottled water

    • Typical night

    11pm/Midnight: Go to bar
    2/3am: Head to a nightclub
    4/5am: Get home

    Travelling

    Travelling within Berlin
  • The Mauer Park Floh Markets (great flea market, lots of performers, a great cultural experience)
  • Parades: Gay Pride at the East Side Gallery in the Summer, Christopher Street Parade
  • Kreuzberg (hip neighborhood)
  • Berlin Beer Festival at Central Park
  • Reichstag Building and Bradenburg Gate (historical site from the Berlin War)
  • Berlin Cathedral

    • Day/Weekend Trips
  • Don't go to Weimar (people are racist, it's not very fun, and it's in the countryside)
  • Hamburg (good Portuguese food, it's a port city, and has a nice nightlife for young people)
  • Austria

    • Transportation

    Public Transportation
    Make use of it! Berlin is too large to just walk around.
    Trains are closed from 1 to 4am - plan accordingly.
    Also, don't talk too loudly on the trains, as they will glare at you

    Walking
    There is a very large bicycle culture in Berlin, so make sure to stay away from the bike lanes.

    Museums

    Museum Island: The program gives students a pass for all five museums on the island.

    Last Minute Tips

    Hair-care
    Germany has hard running water, and makes your hair really rough, so bring hair-care products.
    Flight
    Apply for Max Cade travel scholarship before you go, as they will pay for your flights.
    Safety
    There are a lot of scammers - avoid women and little children asking you to do something. They'll try to take things from you. Don't feel bad about not helping them.
    Communication
    The program buys a phone for you to use during your stay. This will help you keep in contact with your peers in the city/country. To contact back home, look for Wi-Fi and use WhatsApp or Viber on a smartphone.
    Bottled Water
    Bottled water is very expensive, and you typically cannot get tap water for free at restaurants, so make sure you bring a water bottle (it is safe to drink from taps).
